BUSINESS ANALYST

PRAUD group ltd., consulting company

may 2015 - november 2016

saint-petersburg, russia

I was invited to join the PRAUD Group Consulting Ltd as a Business Analyst after 4 months of seeking for a new work owing to a reduction in my Project Administrator position due to the financial crisis in Russia in 2014-2015.

About 20 projects for the implementation and monitoring of automated control systems in Russia's leading industrial businesses were completed by the PRAUD Group Company, which has two locations in Moscow and St. Petersburg.

Konstantin Komaedov, my supervisor, assigned me the task of analyzing and proposing ideas for the development of Project Management and Sales Management procedures. He also had a lack of knowledge in the company's Document Flow process, as well as control over contract execution and account activities.

proficient in:

  • creation and upkeep of processes, desk procedures, and knowledge base articles;

  • project management and consulting;

  • end-to-end process understanding;

  • high level of collaboration;

  • introduction of new concepts.

professional experience

I've created Project Management tools and a Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system in addition to supporting the Committee of Directors with office life support.

The developer, Aleksey Uryadnikov, and I automated and implemented the Project Management and Sales procedures into the internal management 1C:Enterprise system after successful testing.

I attended weekly meetings with project directors and sales managers, who reported to the CEO. Using the automated management web platforms I established, I kept track of job execution for the project teams and sales team in between meetings.

I looked at the existing systems to see how I might optimize the workflow process and regulate transactions on the group's accounts. I was surprised by how much time management spent phoning me to obtain a copy of the contract or even physically going to the document archive to examine the documents while I was conducting the analysis.

I developed an internal document archiving system and integrated it with the internal ERP systems.

It was my responsibility to come up with new budgeting and spending methods for financial and operational reporting, as well as to guarantee that contractual and accounting processes were followed effectively, in compliance with strict company standards.

On a regular basis, it was also necessary to keep resolution of claims and disputes under documents, track of deadlines, invoice payment, and reporting.

main successes and achievements

  • Controlling of payment of outgoing invoices.

  • Providing recommendations on data administration.

  • Understanding and communicating the financial and operational impact of any changes.

  • Auditing incoming bills and project expenses, as well as analyzing the costs of workers' business travels and ensuring that they are in line with corporate standards and budgets.

  • Examining, assessing, and offering recommendations for project teams' business needs, as well as assisting in the construction of solid business solutions.

  • Collaboration with functional teams (Development, Legal, and Maintenance) and other relevant people to assist with overall program delivery.

A difficult task I faced and how I addressed it

I was responsible for automating the Project Management and Selling Management systems that I designed for this organization in one of my prior employment as a Project Administrator.

The aim was to complete the transfer to the new management system without causing project deadlines to be impacted by the new working environment. I just had two months to prepare.

To do so, I needed to swiftly learn a new skill in process description. As a result, I used documentation on the automation of a comparable process, known as "Document Flow," to analogize the Project Management and Selling Management processes. I set out time each day for talking with developers and describing the documentation.

As a consequence, the automation was finished ahead of schedule. This provided me an idea of where I might go for new experiences while still doing my regular duties.

HEAD OF THE ADMINISTRATIVE DEPARTMENT

BALTINTERGROUP Itd. train transport company

november 2016 - july 2017

saint-petersburg, russia

There was a noticeable lack of document management at this tiny train service company with 25 workers that handled customs services and foreign trade.

BaltInterGruppq LLC offered rail transportation services across Russia, the CIS, Europe, and Asia.

Every day, it was dozens of transactions for the transportation of products between the CIS and Russia. For each transaction, a bundle of paperwork had to be drawn up, agreed upon, and signed, on which all of the company's workers' operations were carried out.

There was no procedure for storing and reporting papers prior to my arrival. In the Administrative Department, there was just one person who didn't have enough time to handle everything, including creating and improving the document management system.

proficient in:

  • outstanding management;

  • ongoing training for the team;

  • leadership experiences, both formal and informal.

professional experience

My substantial administrative and documentation support to top management in making decisions was a highly valuable role because the CEO was nearly always out for business meetings.

The construction, development, and maintenance of electronic document management systems for the capacity to oversee business online were among my responsibilities. Bitrix24 software was utilized.

I hired and educated a team of three persons with expertise in administration and document management to ensure that operations ran smoothly. I attended contract discussions and managed financial transactions to ensure that all procedures were successfully integrated.

For the administrative department, I created a KPI system as well as project management tools. I assisted with contract negotiations and financial transactions coordination.

main successes and achievements

  • Accountability for driving standardization and continuous improvement.

  • Taking charge of and guiding the implementation of a new procedure through the use of well-defined action plans.

  • Analysis and feedback collection for smaller or less complex processes and procedures.

  • Using KPI indicators, identify opportunities for continuous process improvement and future investment to increase efficiency and improve employee experience.

What was the hardest decision I made?

When I was hired as the lead clerk at a small transportation firm with a little (no) administrative disarray, I was given carte blanche to get things in order.

I had one employee who was just responsible for all administrative concerns in this organization and was subordinated to working in my team. My investigation of difficulties in the administrative sector revealed that my junior employee was incompetent, and she was the one who consistently made mistakes.

I started teaching my colleague, passing on my expertise of how to handle the document flow. We updated the document registers and developed the framework of document flow across departments thanks to her extensive understanding of the company's internal operations.

However, I was confronted with the most challenging psychotype I had ever encountered in my professional life. After a time, my colleague continued to repeat the same mistakes with increasing regularity... just because she refused to "alter habits."

Because of my expertise as an HR manager, I had no option but to suggest to the CEO that the specialist I could choose be changed.

Furthermore, in addition to the existing role, I was given permission to hire two more people. So I let rid of one person in exchange for three others, and we had a fantastic and productive team!

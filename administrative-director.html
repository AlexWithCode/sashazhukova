Head of the Administrative Department

BaltInterGroup Ltd. Train Transport Company

November 2016 - July 2017

Saint-Petersburg, Russia

This small railroad transport company with 25 employees who provided customs services and foreign trade had a noticeable lack of document management.

Ltd. "BaltInterGrupp" had acted as an integrator between commercial companies, public authorities, foreign partners, aiming to simplify and facilitate the process of registration of foreign trade transactions, as well as various customs agreements. "BaltInterGrupp" LLC provided services on cargo transportation, services of rail transport in Russia, CIS countries, Europe, and Asia.

Our company has developed a system of time management, which enables the delivery of cargo to the regulated time. Thanks to the professional team of logisticians analysts LLC "BaltInterGrupp" calculated the best route for cargo and proposed options with saving time and money. Our company used only the latest technology, with which customers received timely information about the stage of execution of the order at any time.

They carried out dozens of transactions per day for the transportation of goods between the CIS countries and Russia. For each transaction, it was necessary to draw up, agree, and sign a package of documents on the basis of which the activities of all employees of the company were carried out.

Before my arrival, there was no system for storing and reporting documents. There was only one employee in the Administrative Department, who did not have time to fulfill all the duties, not only to create and improve the document management system.

What Was The Hardest Decision I Made

When I was invited to a small transport company, where there was a little (no) administrative chaos, I was given carte blanche in my hands as the lead clerk to put things in order.

I had one employee subordinated to work in my team, who was the only responsible person for all administrative issues in this company. The result of my analysis of problems in the administrative sector was that my subordinate employee is not competent and it is she who makes a lot of stable mistakes.

I began to teach my colleague, to transfer to her my knowledge of how the document flow should be managed. Thanks to her deep knowledge of the internal processes of the company, we modified the registers of documents, built the structure of document flow between departments.

But I was faced with the most difficult psycho type in all the time of my work experience. After a while, my colleague began to make the same mistakes with the same frequency... Because she just didn't want "to change habits." I had no choice but to advise the CEO to change a specialist whom I can select due to my experience as an HR manager.

Moreover, I was allowed to place two more employees in addition to the existing position. So I fired one employee but got three others in return, and we had a great team!

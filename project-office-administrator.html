Project Office Administrator

Ulmart.ru Online Retailer

October 2013 - October 2014

Saint-Petersburg, Russia

While I was working at the Business Competence Center (BCC), the company kept growing and increasing its capacity. New large projects appeared and needed to be professionally managed, it was necessary to solve uncontrolled areas of responsibility. Ulmart launched the creation of a Project Office Department for Project Portfolio management.

In February 2013, a new version of the Ulmart online store was launched. At the end of 2013, the company's sales exceeded $ 1 billion and Ulmart took first place in the rating of the Russian Forbes "20 largest online stores in Russia." In the same year, the company began to diversify its assortment, in addition to equipment for computers and laptops, digital and household appliances, children's goods, and toys.

In the spring of 2014, Ulmart gained a stake in Dream Industries (Zvooq music service and Bookmate book service). Later, the services were integrated with the ulmart.ru platform. In the autumn of the same year, Ulmart bought out 100% of the shares of Sputnikovaya Kompaniya LLC, which manages the No Limit Electronics network (the largest distributor of Tricolor TV).

At the same time after one year of work at the BCC, I got the maximum of my fulfillment and I was supposed to offer my candidacy to the new Project Office as a Project Administrator. This position was one of the vacancies in the new team, led by a professional project manager Ivan Korenkov, who was part of the group of translators into Russian of the Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK® Guide) from the Project Management Institute (PMI).

Professional Experience

As a Project Office Administrator, I did very enjoyable work, which consisted of studying the PMBOK® Guide and applying this knowledge of Project Management in practice in a new Project Office which was being created in real-time.

I have described and developed tools for managing a portfolio of about 10-15 IT projects, the purpose of which was to increase the efficiency of top management and to reduce administrative costs. These were tools for initiating and registering projects, for projects planning and projects reports, for collecting and organizing information on projects, and I also wrote down the archival history of projects and their results.

Every working day I had 3-5 meetings with project managers to discuss and consult the project management process based on current results of IT projects. When I was given to describe the business process of Project Management, the project manager's vision of this process was important to me, so we devoted a lot of time to the business analysis of Project Management on these meetings. Our discussion led me to another difficult task related to Project Financing.

Thus, to my responsibilities was added the development and description of the process of financing projects in conjunction with the Financial Director. Every week, colleagues from the Finance Department came to our Project Office to build a project financing process. The Financial Project Management System took into account the costs spent on IT project resources, and unpredictable payments during the implementation of projects, as well as profit-taking after account taxes, called a margin.

During the whole year of work in the Project Office, it was my inalienable duty to analyze and structure the projects' data for the presentation of reports to the Board of Directors and Investors of Ulmart every week.

Recognized Manifestations of Skills

I believe that creativity is the key to a successful solution.
I would have to notice that one of my skill increase situation happened when I had the ability to find a creative solution in the first working steps.

It happened in one of my previous positions as a Project Administrator, at the largest online store, during the height of the sales season, when I analyzed site capacity metrics and their impact on site working speed.

A developer and I combined a lot of different large databases into one small database. We needed to create some tool for IT project owners, on which basis top-management decisions were made. All that was required of me was some spreadsheet for discussion at daily meetings. But I did a bit more.

My infographic with visualization of information in graphs and diagrams turned out to be so practical that it was shown on monitors in the open space for IT teams, and thanks to my work the daily 15-minute offline meetings were canceled. As the result, it saved the time of 40 employees.

It cost about $437 per month for each top manager.
